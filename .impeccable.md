# Impeccable Design Context — Big Bolão 2026

## Design Context

### Users
Friend groups and families running private World Cup prediction pools together. They open the app during weekends, around match kick-off times, and at social gatherings (watch parties, family dinners). The context is casual and social — sharing excitement, trash-talking friends, celebrating correct predictions. These are **not** hardcore bettors or stats-obsessed users; they want a shared ritual around football that is simple and enjoyable.

**Emotional goals**: Anticipation before matches. Delight when predictions come in. Friendly competitiveness. A sense of belonging to a group.

### Brand Personality
Three words: **refined, social, confident**

The app should feel like it was built by people who love football *and* care about craft — a well-designed product applied to sports, not a sports app that happened to get styled. Think of how a premium fintech app (calm, purposeful, no visual noise) would feel if it were celebrating the World Cup with friends.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Both light and dark modes, respecting system preference. Light mode is primary for daytime/social contexts; dark mode for late-night match sessions.
- **Visual tone**: Understated confidence. Clean layouts with generous whitespace. Typography-driven hierarchy. Restrained use of the brand orange (#FF872C) as a true accent — rare enough to feel meaningful.
- **Color approach**: The existing navy/blue (#065894) and orange (#FF872C) palette is solid. Lean into navy as the dominant surface color in dark mode; keep backgrounds warm-neutral (not cold gray) in light mode. Tint all neutrals slightly toward the brand blue.
- **Typography**: The existing Inter font is too generic. When building new screens, use a condensed or variable display typeface for scores, match stats, and headings (something with sports energy but elegance — not Bebas Neue cliché). Pair with a refined humanist sans for body text. Explore: Barlow Condensed + Karla, Saira + Mulish, or Anybody + Rubik.

### Anti-References
- **Gambling / betting apps** (Bet365, Sportingbet): No heavy greens, gold gradients, neon accents, or aggressive "BET NOW" CTA energy. No visual noise competing for attention.
- **Generic social apps** (Facebook, Instagram): No algorithmic-feed aesthetics, no blue-on-white corporate palettes, no thumbs-scroll infinite lists with no hierarchy.

### Design Principles
1. **Friends first, sports second** — the social connection is the product. Leaderboards and predictions exist to fuel conversation, not replace it.
2. **Restraint with the accent** — orange is precious. Use it for the one thing a user needs to act on. Everything else is navy and warm neutral.
3. **Score as art** — match scores and predictions are the hero data. Typography and layout should make them feel significant, not like form fields.
4. **No gambling energy** — zero tolerance for neon, aggressive CTAs, green-gold, or anything that makes a casual user feel like they wandered into a bookmaker's shop.
5. **Works at a glance** — users are watching a match and want a 3-second check. Hierarchy must be brutal and clear. Never bury the key info.
